### 🤖 Claude

Now precise. You have **two** walk-forward setups, different answers each:

## 1. `run_walkforward.py` — rolling-origin CV
Pure **evaluation**. Slides test window back N folds, scores each, reports PR-AUC mean±std. Model trained same way every fold. Does **not** improve accuracy. Buys you:
- honest temporal estimate (single holdout = 1 lucky/unlucky draw; folds = variance + worst-case)
- drift visibility across war regimes

So: better/truer test results, not better model. Correct — that's its job.

## 2. `src/retrain.py` — walk-forward **retrain**
This one **does** improve real accuracy. Score-then-adapt: when drift fires (PSI > threshold), refits on trailing window, new model governs next block. [retrain.py:120-137](src/retrain.py#L120-L137).

`compare_policies` measures exactly your question: `never` (frozen) vs `drift`/`periodic` (adaptive). Gap = accuracy gained by retraining. [retrain.py:151](src/retrain.py#L151). Lower mean pinball = adaptive wins.

## Bottom line
- **CV** → measures, no accuracy gain. Honest number.
- **Retrain harness** → real gain, IF `drift` beats `never` in `compare_policies`.

Two caveats on "real gain":
1. Gain is conditional — only if non-stationary. Stationary data → frozen ties adaptive, retrain just burns compute.
2. CV indirectly helps deployed model too: pick hyperparams that generalize temporally, not to one split. Indirect, not the mechanism itself.
