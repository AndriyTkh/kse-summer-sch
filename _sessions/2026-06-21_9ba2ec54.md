Confirmed root cause. Edge row at 19:00: `hours_since_alert=17`, short-window recency quiet, all `tempo_*`/threat features `NaN` (data ran out 17-43h ago).

## What forecast script supposed to do

[run_forecast.py](scripts/runs/run_forecast.py) = product output, not backtest. Train Model B on all data, fit isotonic on last 4 weeks, predict **latest grid hour per oblast** → next-30m/1h/3h/6h alert prob. Write [nowcast.json](viz/public/nowcast.json).

## Why empty (~all 0.0)

Grid end anchored to **wall-clock now**, data feeds lag behind:

| feed | freshest | behind now (19:00) |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| alerts | 2026-06-21 01:07 | ~18h |
| waves | 2026-06-20 15:00 | ~28h |
| daily | 2026-06-20 00:00 | ~43h |

Source: [index.py:33-34](src/index.py#L33-L34) — `end` defaults to `now().floor('1h')`.

Forecast origin = 19:00 sits in **dead zone past every feed's edge**. Model's discriminative power lives in short recency + threat-launch features. At edge:
- `hours_since_alert = 17`, short windows (1h/3h/6h) quiet
- all `tempo_*` / threat cols = `NaN` (as-of join finds nothing recent)

So model correctly says "~0 chance next 6h given 17h silence + no live launch data", then isotonic squashes further → near-zero everywhere.

Not a leak/logic bug. Exactly the **ragged-right-edge caveat** the script docstrings warn about ([forecast.py:9-14](src/forecast.py#L9-L14)) — but worse than "partial": threat feed fully NaN, not just understated.

Why other files look fine: [predictions.json](viz/public/predictions.json) + [onset.json](viz/public/onset.json) are **backtests over held-out historical windows** (real rows with activity), not the live edge.

## Fix

Anchor grid `end` to the **true ragged edge** = min(data-max across feeds), not `now()`. Predict the latest hour where feeds actually have data (~01:00, capped by slowest feed if threat matters). That's likely what your new [scripts/data_freshness.py](scripts/data_freshness.py) + [scripts/update_data.py](scripts/update_data.py) target.
